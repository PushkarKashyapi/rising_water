Global

Between 1990 and 2022, global floods caused over $1.3 trillion in economic losses, affecting more than 3.2 billion people worldwide. A total of 4,713 flood events were recorded across 168 countries. These floods led to over 218,000 deaths, showing the devastating human impact. Developing nations often face the worst outcomes due to poor infrastructure and limited disaster response systems.

State-wise

Certain states consistently face the brunt of flooding due to their geography and climate. West Bengal leads with ₹16,079 crore in damage, followed by Karnataka (₹9,833 crore), Tamil Nadu (₹7,040 crore), and Andhra Pradesh (₹6,320 crore). Maharashtra also saw about ₹4,000 crore in damages in 2021 alone. These figures highlight where mitigation efforts and infrastructure investment are most needed.

Seasonal

Flood damage in India follows a predictable monthly pattern, peaking during the monsoon season from June to September. These months bring heavy rainfall that causes rivers to overflow, leading to urban and rural flooding. Historical data shows major flood events almost always coincide with these months. This seasonal trend is vital for disaster planning and early warning systems.

India

India is one of the most flood-affected countries, suffering over $115 billion in damages since 1990. More than 629 million people have been affected and over 46,000 lives lost. On average, floods cause India ₹25,000 crore in losses each year, with spikes during extreme events. In 2021 alone, the damage reached ₹49,618 crore (~$6.2 billion).
